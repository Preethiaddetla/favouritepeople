Viswanathan Anand

Viswanathan Anand, often referred to as "Vishy," is one of the greatest chess players in history and a trailblazer for Indian chess. Born on December 11, 1969, in Chennai, India, Anand rose to prominence in the 1980s and 1990s, earning the title of Grandmaster in 1988. Known for his speed and intuitive play, he became the first Indian to win the FIDE World Chess Championship in 2000. Anand's career is marked by his versatility, as he has successfully competed in both classical and rapid chess formats. He reclaimed the World Chess Championship title in 2007 and defended it in several subsequent matches, solidifying his place among the chess elite. Anand's contributions to the game extend beyond his achievements on the board; he has inspired a new generation of chess players in India and around the world. His calm demeanor, strategic brilliance, and sportsmanship have earned him widespread respect and admiration in the global chess community.

Anirudh Ravichander

Anirudh Ravichander is an acclaimed Indian music composer and singer, predominantly working in the Tamil film industry. Born on October 16, 1990, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, Anirudh shot to fame with his debut song "Why This Kolaveri Di" from the movie *3* in 2011. The song became a viral sensation, garnering widespread recognition and establishing him as a musical prodigy. Known for his innovative compositions that blend traditional Indian music with contemporary styles, Anirudh has become a household name in the South Indian music industry. His work spans various genres, from upbeat dance numbers to soulful melodies, making him one of the most sought-after composers in the industry. Beyond Tamil cinema, Anirudh has also worked in Telugu and Hindi films, further expanding his influence and reach. His energetic performances and unique sound have earned him numerous awards and a massive fan following across India and beyond.

Sudha Murty

Sudha Murthy is an eminent Indian author and philanthropist, known for her contributions to literature and social work. Born on August 19, 1950, in Karnataka, she is the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation and has played a crucial role in supporting education, healthcare, and rural development in India. A prolific writer, Sudha Murthy has authored numerous books in English and Kannada, often drawing on themes of compassion, simplicity, and social justice. Her impactful work in both literature and philanthropy has earned her widespread respect and recognition, including the Padma Shri award.

Kapil Sharma

Kapil Sharma is a celebrated Indian comedian, television host, and actor, best known for his hit show *The Kapil Sharma Show*. Born on April 2, 1981, in Amritsar, Punjab, he rose to fame after winning *The Great Indian Laughter Challenge* in 2007. Kapil's sharp wit and relatable humor have made him one of India's most beloved entertainers, earning him a massive following. His comedy often reflects everyday life, making it accessible and enjoyable for a wide audience. Despite ups and downs in his career, Kapil remains a prominent figure in Indian comedy, known for bringing laughter to millions.

Rowan Atkinson

Rowan Atkinson is a highly acclaimed British actor and comedian, best known for his portrayal of the iconic character Mr. Bean. Born on January 6, 1955, in England, Atkinson's career took off with his work on the sketch show *Not the Nine O'Clock News*, but it was Mr. Bean that brought him international fame. His unique style of physical comedy and expressive performance made the mostly silent character beloved by audiences around the world. Beyond Mr. Bean, Atkinson is also celebrated for his role as the witty Edmund Blackadder in the sitcom *Blackadder*. His enduring influence in comedy has made him one of the most respected figures in the entertainment industry.
